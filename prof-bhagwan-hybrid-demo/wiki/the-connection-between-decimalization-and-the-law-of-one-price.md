# The Connection Between Decimalization And The Law Of One Price

The connection between decimalization and the Law of One Price is underexplored: tighter spreads should theoretically enforce price uniformity across venues, yet the thinning of quoted depth post-decimalization created new forms of price dispersion for large orders — a subtle violation of the spirit of the Law of One Price at institutional scale. Additionally, the parallel to foreign exchange transaction cost structure is illuminating: FX markets have always operated with extremely tight spreads but significant market impact for large trades, making the post-decimalization equity market more FX-like in its microstructure. This connection between equity decimalization and FX market cost architecture is a novel synthesis worth preserving.
