# What Is The Law Of One Price In Finance?

A productive connection emerges between the Law of One Price and the Gravity Model of financial transaction costs. Where the Law of One Price assumes competitive, frictionless markets, the gravity model explains precisely *why* the law breaks down in practice for underserved populations — distance, information asymmetry, and institutional gaps create price wedges for identical financial products across geographies. This links the canonical no-arbitrage framework in Berk & DeMarzo directly to financial inclusion architecture: violations of the Law of One Price in credit and savings markets are a quantifiable measure of financial exclusion.
