# FAB, Blockchain Identity, and Threshold Behavior

This page synthesizes three interconnected concepts into a unified framework for understanding how universal financial inclusion can be achieved within a single generation.

## The Core Synthesis

Traditional financial inclusion fails because it attacks households one at a time, fighting against network effects at every step. FAB combined with blockchain-based self-sovereign identity restructures the problem entirely -- from a household-by-household battle to a cohort-level threshold-crossing event.

## Layer 1: FAB as Incentive Mechanism

FAB connects three proven innovations: a $100 savings deposit for every newborn, a unique universal ID at birth, and mobile/electronic branchless banking. The $100 is not primarily welfare -- it is a mechanism design tool that makes formal registration rationally self-interested for parents. Total cost: $10 billion annually (100 million FAB babies x $100), or 1/50th of 1% of world GDP.

## Layer 2: Blockchain Identity as Infrastructure Upgrade

Traditional centralized identity (even Aadhaar) has single points of failure, biometric mismatch vulnerabilities, and government control risks. Blockchain-based self-sovereign identity offers:
- Immutable birth records unfalsifiable on-chain
- Child owns their identity, not the state
- Globally interoperable across borders
- Cost potentially under $1 per person

Combined with FAB, the account becomes a globally portable financial identity rather than merely a local bank account.

## Layer 3: Threshold Behavior Dynamics

Financial inclusion scales through thresholds, not linearly:
- At ~5% inclusion: system is irrelevant due to absent network effects
- At ~30% inclusion: critical mass is reached; merchants accept digital payments, savings groups form
- At ~60%+ inclusion: irreversibility threshold -- exclusion becomes socially costly

## The Leapfrog Mechanism

FAB + Blockchain Identity achieves simultaneous universal enrollment for an entire birth cohort. Rather than fighting threshold behavior household by household, it leapfrogs the threshold entirely. Within 20 years, a full cohort of young adults exists who have never known financial exclusion -- with transaction histories, credit accessibility, and insurable risk profiles.

## Systemic Risk Considerations

Blockchain identity at scale introduces new systemic risks:
- Protocol vulnerabilities could compromise billions of identities simultaneously
- No undo mechanism exists on-chain
- Concentration in one or two protocols creates catastrophic correlation risk

This makes the SHUb design standard (Simple, Human, Ubiquitous) critical -- systems must be simple enough for rural populations, human enough for failure recovery, and ubiquitous enough to cross inclusion thresholds.
